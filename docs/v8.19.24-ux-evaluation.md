## What the Channel card currently shows

### Channel heading (renderTaskGroup)
- State dot (green/grey/amber)
- "CHANNEL" label
- Title (from seed -> mission.md -> boss nick fallback)
- Boss nick (amber text)
- Worker count ("2 workers")
- Token total (blue badge, e.g. "19k")

### Room card (renderChannelCard)
- Room name (`#boss`, `#team`, `#task-<worker>`)
- Category tag (BOSS / SHARED / WORKER)
- Per-room token total
- Seed brief toggle (collapsible, lazy-fetched) -- only when seed exists
- Member chips: state dot + nick + role badge + per-member token count

### What clicking does
- Click member chip -> opens that agent's Activity stream
- Click card body -> opens that room's Chat, selects the worker agent

## Ranked Findings

### Finding 1 (Critical): No timeline information -- when did work start? When was last activity?

**SYMPTOM:** The operator cannot tell whether a channel has been active for 5 minutes or 5 hours. There is no "started at", "last activity", or "elapsed time" anywhere on the card. A green dot means "process is running" but says nothing about recency of actual work.

**ROOT CAUSE:** `list_tasks()` in `server.py` does not compute or return any timestamp fields. The `_last_daemon_action()` helper exists (line 166) but is only used by the Agents tab, not by the task-grouped Channels view. No equivalent field is included in the task or channel dicts returned by `/api/tasks`.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Add `started_at` (first `agent_start` in daemon-log) and `last_activity` (most recent entry in daemon-log or usage-log) to each member dict in `list_tasks()`. Render on the Channel heading as "Started 2h ago -- last active 3m ago" using a `timeAgo()` formatter. Also add `last_activity` per room card (most recent message timestamp from the IRC channel).

---

### Finding 2 (Critical): No task purpose/description visible at a glance

**SYMPTOM:** The Channel heading shows a truncated title like "You ..." or "loc...". The seed brief exists only on individual WORKER room cards, not on the Channel heading itself. An operator scanning the list cannot answer "what is this team doing?" without expanding a seed brief toggle inside a specific room card.

**ROOT CAUSE:** The `task.title` in `renderTaskGroup()` is a single-line string derived from the first seed found across workers, or mission.md, or the boss nick. It's rendered with CSS `overflow: hidden; text-overflow: ellipsis` and truncates aggressively. There is no summary line or description field on the Channel heading.

**PROPOSED FIX:** (a) Show the full title (or at least 2 lines) on the Channel heading -- remove single-line truncation. (b) Add a Channel-level brief summary: render the first 1-2 sentences of the living brief (`/api/channels/<name>/brief`) as a subtitle under the Channel heading. (c) If no brief exists, show the seed preview at the Channel level, not just per-room.

---

### Finding 3 (High): No health/stuck indicators

**SYMPTOM:** The only health signal is the state dot (green = PID alive, grey = stopped). There is no visual warning when a worker has been silent for a long time, is in a crash loop, or is stuck retrying. The watcher daemon (v8.19.19) detects these patterns but does not surface them in the dashboard.

**ROOT CAUSE:** The watcher runs out-of-process and alerts via IRC/email/webhook. There is no API endpoint to query watcher alerts or current health status. The dashboard has no integration with watcher findings.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Add `GET /api/watcher/alerts` endpoint that returns active (non-expired) alerts per agent. Render an alert badge (red/amber) on the Channel heading or member chip when the watcher has flagged a pattern (e.g. "crash_burst", "mission_stuck"). Tooltip shows the alert message.

---

### Finding 4 (High): No pending-permissions-per-channel indicator

**SYMPTOM:** The "PENDING APPROVALS" panel in the right column shows a global list. There is no per-channel or per-worker count on the Channel card. An operator must scan the global list to figure out which team is blocked waiting for approval.

**ROOT CAUSE:** `_pending_counts()` in `server.py` (line 156) computes per-agent pending counts, and the Agents tab renders them (`a.pending > 0` at app.js line 173). But `list_tasks()` does not include pending counts in its member dicts, and `renderChannelCard()` / `renderTaskGroup()` do not render them.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Include `pending_count` in each member dict returned by `list_tasks()`. Render a "2 pending" badge (amber/red) on the Channel heading when any member has pending approvals. This lets operators instantly see which teams are blocked.

---

### Finding 5 (Medium): No living brief preview on Channel cards

**SYMPTOM:** The living brief (v8.19.24) exists and has an API endpoint (`/api/channels/<name>/brief`), but the Channel card renders no preview or toggle for it. Only the seed brief (write-once) has a toggle. The living brief -- the running onboarding doc that grows as work progresses -- is invisible from the dashboard unless you manually call the API.

**ROOT CAUSE:** `renderChannelCard()` has a seed-brief toggle block (lines 368-402) but no equivalent for the living brief. `list_tasks()` does not return a `brief_preview` or `has_brief` field.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Add `has_brief: true/false` and optionally `brief_preview` (first 100 chars) to each channel dict in `list_tasks()`. Render a "Living brief" toggle alongside the seed brief toggle on room cards. On the Channel heading, show "Brief available" indicator when any room has a living brief.

---

### Finding 6 (Medium): Channel title truncation hides critical context

**SYMPTOM:** In the screenshot, the first channel shows "You ..." and the second shows "loc..." -- both are truncated to the point of being useless. The title is the primary way an operator identifies what a channel is for.

**ROOT CAUSE:** CSS on `.task-title` uses `overflow: hidden; text-overflow: ellipsis; white-space: nowrap` which clips at the container width. The Channel heading also packs label + boss + worker count + tokens into one row, leaving little space for the title.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Allow the title to wrap to 2 lines (`white-space: normal; -webkit-line-clamp: 2; display: -webkit-box; -webkit-box-orient: vertical`). Alternatively, move the boss nick and worker count to a second line below the title so the title gets the full row width.

---

### Finding 7 (Low): No cost conversion for tokens

**SYMPTOM:** Token counts ("19k", "6.0k") are shown but the operator cannot quickly estimate dollar cost. For budget-conscious runs this means mental math or checking pricing tables.

**ROOT CAUSE:** `formatTokens()` only converts to human-readable token counts. No cost model is applied.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Optional: add a `formatCost()` that estimates cost based on model pricing (configurable). Show as tooltip on the token badge: "19k tokens (~$0.12)".

---

### Finding 8 (Low): Second channel group lacks token badge entirely

**SYMPTOM:** The `local-boss2` channel group shows "0 WORKERS" and no token badge. This is technically correct (no workers = no tokens) but visually inconsistent -- the absence of the badge makes it look broken rather than zero.

**ROOT CAUSE:** `renderTaskGroup()` line 320: `if (task.tokens_total && task.tokens_total > 0)` -- the badge is hidden when total is 0 or falsy.

**PROPOSED FIX:** Consider showing "0" or a dash for zero-token channels to maintain visual consistency, or add a subtle "no activity" label.

## Overall Conclusion

The user's complaint ("hardly any information here about the channel") is validated. The Channel card is **identity-rich but context-poor**:

**What it does well:**
- Shows WHO is in each channel (member chips with state dots and role badges)
- Shows token COSTS per member, per room, and per channel
- Shows the structural layout (boss/shared/worker rooms)
- Provides seed brief access via lazy-loaded toggle

**What it lacks (ordered by operator impact):**
1. **WHEN** -- no timestamps, no "last active", no duration. The operator is timeline-blind.
2. **WHAT** -- title truncation and no brief preview mean the operator can't tell what the task is about without clicking into individual rooms.
3. **HOW (health)** -- no stuck/crash/stall indicators surfaced from the watcher. Green dot = "alive" but not "healthy."
4. **BLOCKED** -- no per-channel pending-approval count. Operator must scan the global panel.
5. **PROGRESS** -- no living brief preview. The brief exists but is hidden from the card surface.

The top 4 findings (timeline, purpose, health, pending) would transform the Channel card from "I can see who's here and how much they've spent" to "I can tell at a glance whether this team is healthy, active, and on track."
